About the RiskIQ Model
Technical documentation for the underlying AI architecture
🤖 Model Architecture: LightGBM
Gradient Boosting Decision Tree (GBDT)

The RiskIQ Dashboard is powered by LightGBM, a high-performance, distributed gradient boosting framework developed by Microsoft. It is specifically designed for speed and efficiency when handling large-scale tabular data.

Key Technical Features:

  • Leaf-wise Tree Growth: Unlike most GBDT frameworks that grow trees level-wise, LightGBM grows trees leaf-wise. This strategy chooses the leaf with max delta loss to grow, resulting in lower loss and higher accuracy.
  • GOSS (Gradient-based One-Side Sampling): A novel technique that identifies data instances with larger gradients to contribute more to information gain, significantly reducing computation time without sacrificing precision.
  • EFB (Exclusive Feature Bundling): Bundles sparse, mutually exclusive features to reduce the dimensionality of the feature space, improving training speed.

Why LightGBM for Loan Risk?

Loan assessment requires a balance of interpretability and high precision. LightGBM excels at capturing non-linear relationships between financial variables (like the interaction between DTI ratio and Credit History) that traditional linear models often miss.

Optimization Pillar

The model utilizes Histogram-based decision trees, which group continuous feature values into discrete bins. This significantly reduces memory usage and accelerates training by up to 15x compared to standard GBDT.

🔍 Feature Taxonomy
Understanding the signals that drive assessments
Financial Solvency
Covers Income, Loan Amount, and Debt-to-Income (DTI). This is the primary driver for repayment capacity.
Inherent Credit Risk
Derived from Credit Score and historical repayment behavior. Highly predictive of future defaults.
Structural Stability
Employment Type and Months Employed. Gauges the longevity and reliability of income streams.
🚀 Growth Strategy

Leaf-wise vs Level-wise: Standard models grow trees row by row. RiskIQ's LightGBM grows by identifying the paths that reduce error most aggressively (Leaf-wise). While this requires more careful hyperparameter tuning (like max_depth), it allows the system to capture much more complex, non-linear relationships.

⚖️ Ethical AI Pipeline

Our pipeline strictly excludes protected classes (gender, age in sensitive contexts, ethnicity) during feature engineering. We use SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) during validation to ensure that the model relies on legitimate financial factors for its decisions.
